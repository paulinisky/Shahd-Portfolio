About

Shahd started off with an outdated camcorder at the age of 8 filming what are now nostalgic home videos. She took interest in creating professional films in high school after joining the Audio Video production course which introduced her to the art of cinema. Shahd has worked on over 20 projects in the past 3 years. She is currently attending Houston Community College in hopes of gaining a degree in filmmaking by the end of 2021.

Shahd lives with Cystic Fibrosis which has made following her dream difficult, but she never stopped. She was able to receive a double lung transplant at the end of 2019 with her Islamic beliefs being her ultimate motivation. Her script “Keep Breathing” is based on her own life experiences of a young girl who receives the call for her new lungs. By using her platform, Shahd’s goal is to be the voice of those who are unrecognized and unheard.

She won two awards in her high school’s film festival for two of her shorts; Most entertaining with best acting and 3rd place advertisement winner in 2016. Shahd also filmed and directed the school uniform commercial that was presented to the entire school the same year. She was a news anchor for her school’s journalism program. Shahd likes to go above and beyond to get magnificent results in her films. She has a passion for painting and feels it has a strong connection to film. Both are a process that is rewarding to see in its completed state.
